MY LEARNING HIGHLIGHTS IN THE SUBJECT

Diving into the intricate workings of computer components has been a thoroughly enjoyable and enlightening learning experience for me. Simultaneously, my foray into web development has added another layer to this journey. Mastering HTML and CSS has provided me with the tools to not just write lines of code but to actively shape the virtual landscape. Understanding how these languages contribute to the structure and style of websites has empowered me to play a meaningful role in the ever-evolving digital realm. This dual exploration has not only equipped me with valuable technical skills but has also allowed me to bridge the realms of hardware and web development, creating a more comprehensive understanding of the technology that surrounds us.

The HTML & CSS was my favourite lesson especially as i mentioned it played a great role in a website, one of my dream was to be a web developer.

LEARNING 1:

Between high and low-level programming languages by highlighting that high-level languages are more abstract and user-friendly, often resembling human language, while low-level languages provide more direct control over hardware and are closer to machine code. Additionally, emphasize that high-level languages offer portability and easier maintenance, whereas low-level languages enable precise optimization but demand more expertise and effort

LEARNING 2:

In my exploration of Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), I’ve delved into understanding the fundamental principles that govern the interaction between humans and digital systems. This includes studying user-centered design, usability, accessibility, and the integration of psychology and design to create interfaces that enhance user experience. I’ve also gained practical insights into prototyping, user testing, and iterative design processes, aiming to create intuitive and efficient interactions between users and technology.

LEARNING 3:

In my learning about information, I’ve discovered a cycle that turns raw facts into useful knowledge. First, we collect data from different sources. Then, we process it to make sense of it. After that, we share the organized information with others. We also store this info for later use and consider feedback for improvements. It’s important to know that raw facts are called data, and when we organize them, they become useful information. The process involves collecting, preparing, inputting, processing, outputting, storing, and retrieving data. Understanding this cycle helps me make sense of how information flows and how data turns into helpful knowledge.

LEARNING 4:

Learning machine learning is like understanding various tools for solving problems with data. It’s broad. Neural networks, a part of that, are like a special tool inspired by the brain, useful for more complex tasks. So, you’ve learned both the basics and a special advanced tool for specific challenges.
